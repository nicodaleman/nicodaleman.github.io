About

Nico Daleman is an audio engineer, musicologist and sound artist based in Berlin. His works and compositions focus on new techniques for sound spatialization, digital signal processes and algorithmic composition in order to create and interpret real and virtual spaces. His practice relies on computer programing to create digital instruments, audiovisual performances, sound installations and multichannel composition. His research fields focus on musical performance practice and its interaction with new technologies in the areas of academic and experimental electronic music, mixed music and sound art. As his artistic practice and theoretical interest juxtapose in the application of computational techniques for musical analysis such as Music Information Retrieval and Machine Listening.

His pieces have been shown in Haus der Kulturen der Welt, CLB at Aufbau Haus, SoundSAbout, modular+space at Greenhouse, among others and he has taken part in performances at Zentrum für Kunst und Medien in Karlsruhe, Darmstadt 2018, ACUD MACHT NEU and Loophole among many others. He has participated in summer courses and academies such as GUSAC in Mainz 2019, Darmstadt 2018, Manifeste 2017 as an auditor, and has taken classes with Bernhard Leitner, Cristina Kubisch, Hans Peter Kuhn, Diemo Schwarz, Chris Chafe, Mikhail Malt among others. He is also an active contributor to Cashmere Radio, both as a host and as sound engineer and designer.

He holds a B.M. Audio Engineer and a M.M in Musicology. Currently he works for the classical music streaming app IDAGIO as a music cataloguer and is enrolled in the Master program of Sound Studies and Sonic Arts at UdK Berlin.
